What Is a Wells Notice?

A Wells Notice, also known as a preliminary Wells Submission, is an official letter sent by the SEC to people or businesses that are the subject of an inquiry. It is a warning that possible breaches of securities laws may result in civil enforcement action being considered by the SEC’s enforcement team. This notice, which bears the name of John Wells, a former director of the SEC’s Enforcement Division, gives recipients a chance to reply before official charges are brought.

Why Did Brook Taube Receive a Wells Notice?

The SEC’s inquiry into Medley Management Inc. is directly related to Brook Taube’s receipt of a Wells Notice. The investigation is centered on possible violations of securities laws, specifically with reference to Medley’s business practices and compliance with legal requirements. Reports of conflicts of interest and overcharging customers have surfaced, leading the SEC to thoroughly examine Medley’s business operations.
