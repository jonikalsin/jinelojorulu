How does the Seminole Tribe feel about FSU?

August 11, 2024
blog

The partnership between Florida State University (FSU) and the Seminole Tribe of Florida is a unique and collaborative one that has spanned decades. FSU honors the Seminole Tribe by calling themselves the "Seminoles" and acknowledges the responsibility and honor entrusted to them by the Tribe. In this article, we will explore how the Seminole Tribe feels about FSU and their use of the Seminole name and imagery.

Historical Background

FSU students chose the name "Seminole" for their football team in 1947. However, the university's early representations of Seminole imagery were disrespectful to Native Americans. Recognizing this, FSU and the Seminole Tribe began a partnership in the 1970s to address these concerns and ensure that the use of the Seminole name and iconography aligns with the Tribe's values. In 2005, the Tribal Council of the Seminole Tribe of Florida approved a resolution supporting FSU's use of the Seminole name and imagery, further solidifying their partnership.

Collaboration and Approval

The Seminole Tribe works closely with FSU to ensure that the use of the Seminole name and iconography is respectful and appropriate. They collaborate on various issues of mutual interest and representatives of the Tribe serve as dignitaries at university functions. The Seminole Tribe has also approved regalia and consulted on FSU's uniforms, showing their active involvement in shaping the university's representation of the Seminole culture. This collaboration is viewed as a partnership that provides educational opportunities and promotes the Seminole Tribe's history and spirit.

Solidarity and Identity

FSU's use of the Seminole name and imagery is seen as a tribute to the Tribe's resilience. It forms a sense of solidarity between FSU and the Seminole Tribe, with the Tribe's power inspiring all activities at the university. The Seminole name is different from other names used in sports teams, as FSU uses it with explicit tribal permission. The Seminole Tribe sees FSU's use of their name as unique and distinct, emphasizing the cultural and historical significance it holds for both parties.

Ongoing Discussions

While FSU and the Seminole Tribe have established a strong partnership, there are ongoing discussions about cultural appropriation. The evolving relationship between FSU and the Seminole Tribe reflects a growing awareness of the need for cultural sensitivity and respect in the representation of Native American traditions and identities. These discussions highlight the importance of ongoing dialogue and collaboration between FSU and the Seminole Tribe to ensure that the university's use of the Seminole name and imagery remains respectful and in alignment with the Tribe's values.

The Seminole Tribe of Florida holds a positive and collaborative view of FSU's use of the Seminole name and imagery. The partnership between FSU and the Seminole Tribe dates back decades and is based on mutual respect and collaboration. While ongoing discussions about cultural appropriation exist, the Seminole Tribe views their relationship with FSU as a collaboration that provides educational opportunities and promotes the Seminole Tribe's history and spirit. Through this partnership, FSU pays homage to the resilience of the Seminole Tribe and fosters a sense of solidarity between the university and the Tribe.
