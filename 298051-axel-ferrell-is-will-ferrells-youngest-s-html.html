Many people are curious to know whether or not Axel Ferrell, son of renowned comedian and actor Will Ferrell, will follow in his father's footsteps in the world of comedy and entertain millions of people.

Being the son of famous comedian Will Ferrell, Axel Ferrell has been a fascinating personality for Hollywood fans. On several occasions, his dad has had glowing praise for their family.

The famous comedian shares Axel with his wife of many years, Viveca Paulin. Axel's father is notable for his funny antics on TV and the big screen, so it may not be surprising to know that the young man is growing up in a playful home.

The Birth of Axel

Will and Viveca welcomed their son Axel on January 23, 2010, in Los Angeles. The fact that the young boy's father was a comedian seemed like both a blessing and a curse. The comedy actor added:

"I can only imagine, but I sometimes equate [parenting boys] to what it must be like running a prison."

The comedy actor has stated publicly that one of his "greatest joys" as a parent was embarrassing Axel and his siblings, especially when they misbehave or did something wrong.

Will Ferrell and his son Axel Ferrell watch a basketball game between the Brooklyn Nets and the Los Angeles Lakers at Staples Center on February 23, 2014, in Los Angeles, California. | Source: Getty Images

He said that he was trying to warn his children by being annoying. He explained that every time they made rude comments, he would show up at dinner wearing nothing but his underpants.

Although Will earlier admitted to embarrassing his children when they misbehaved, the tables would turn when it came to following family traditions.

The doting dad joked about how often he found himself "barking orders" to his children about what to do. He went on to say there was so much chaos that he didn't have time to articulate his thoughts.

Both of Axel's Parents Have Acting Credits

Axel's parents are well-known in the entertainment industry. Viveca first met her future husband in an acting class in 1995, and they went on to date. Will awkwardly proposed to Viveca years later, and the couple tied the knot in 2000.

Axel's father gained fame through comedy films such as "Anchorman: The Legend of Burgundy," "Elf," and "Daddy's Home." He is best known for his funny antics on "Saturday Night Live."

Will Ferrell and Viveca Paulin attend The 63rd Annual Golden Globe Awards at The Beverly Hilton on January 16, 2006, in Beverly Hills, California. | Source: Getty Images

Meanwhile, Viveca is an art collector and auctioneer, but she also has acting experience. She appeared in Will's movie "A Night at the Roxbury" and as Anton Bjornberg's wife in TV Land's "Younger" series.

The power couple has been together for over two decades. Will gushed about Viveca and said she was perfect for him because she understood his sense of humor.

Axel Is the Youngest of the Siblings

Magnus, born on March 7, 2004, and Mattias, born on December 30, 2006, are Axel's older brothers. His brothers were given Scandinavian names in honor of their mother's Swedish heritage.

There's not much information about Axel's teenage brothers. However, Magnus recently entered the dating scene, while Mattias has developed an interest in sports, especially soccer.

Will Ferrell, his wife Viveca Paulin, and their sons Mattias and Magnus Ferrell attend the 2014 US Open at USTA Billie Jean King National Tennis Center on August 30, 2014, in New York City. | Source: Getty Images

Viveca Paulin and her husband prefer to keep their children out of the spotlight, but the veteran comedian occasionally discusses them publicly. He said his boys have picked up on his funny sense of humor.

Although Will earlier admitted to embarrassing his children when they misbehaved, the tables would turn when it came to following family traditions.

Every Halloween, the actor said, Axel and his brothers would choose his costume no matter what it was. He shared that his boys had once picked out a poop emoji costume for him to wear for the occasion.

Will Ferrell, his wife Viveca Paulin, and their sons Magnus, Mattias, and Axel Ferrell attend the Regency Village Theatre premiere of Paramount Pictures' "Daddy's Home 2" on November 5, 2017, in Westwood, California. | Source: Getty Images

It's uncertain whether Axel and his siblings will go into show business, but the boys once tried their hand at acting when they all landed roles in their father's film "Anchorman 2: The Legend Continues."

Will has said that he values spending time with his kids at home or at his workplace. If he learned anything about being a parent, keeping the lines of communication open was the best way to stay close to his children.
